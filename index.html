Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as M31, is the nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way. It contains approximately one trillion stars and is on a collision course with our galaxy, expected to merge in about 4.5 billion years.

The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star at the center of our solar system. It provides energy that sustains life on Earth and is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Milky Way Galaxy

The Milky Way Galaxy is our home galaxy, a barred spiral structure containing billions of stars, planets, and nebulae. It is approximately 100,000 light-years across.
